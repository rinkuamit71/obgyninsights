Oleoylethanolamide Reduces Dysmenorrhea Pain and Oxidative Stress in Girls :

In this study, girls with primary dysmenorrhea were used to assess how oleoylethanolamide (OEA) affected oxidative stress and inflammatory markers. The patients were divided into two groups at random and given either a placebo or a pill containing 125 mg of OEA daily (n = 22). At the start and conclusion of the experiment, researchers assessed the subjects' levels of pain intensity, total antioxidant capacity (TAC), malondialdehyde (MDA), C-reactive protein (CRP), and tumour necrosis factor alpha (TNF-). In comparison to the placebo group, oral OEA intake for 60 days substantially enhanced TAC (p = 0.022) and reduced menstrual discomfort. The usage of this supplement can be introduced as an alternative therapy to minimise the use of anti-inflammatory medicines because OEA has been shown to diminish menstrual discomfort. The differences between the factors under examination at the beginning and the conclusion of the study were not statistically significant.

Increased Calorie Intake to Reverse Energy Deficiency in Exercising Women: Impact on Bone and Menstrual Cyclicity

The randomised controlled REFUEL study examined how exercising women with menstrual irregularities affected bone health and menstrual function. Oligomenorrhea/amenorrhea (Oligo/Amen) - afflicted women were randomised to either the intervention group, who increased energy intake by 20% to 40% over baseline for a 12-month period, or the control group, which which did not. In contrast to the control group, increasing dietary energy intake increased weight, body fat, and menstruation frequency, but did not enhance bone mineral density. The 12 month intervention could have been too brief, because the increase in calorie intake wasn't enough to boost oestrogen levels, menstruation frequency, or a BMD. Results: Over the course of a year, exercising women with oligomenorrhea/amenorrhea showed improvements in their energy status, body mass (2.6 0.4 kg), femoral neck aBMD, and insulin-like growth factor 1 (37.4 14.6 ng/mL). At month 6, both groups showed a decline in total hip areal bone mineral density (aBMD) and a higher chance of menstruation (P 0.05).

Music and Premenstrual Symptoms and Quality of Life

The goal of this study was to demonstrate how music therapy may improve women's quality of life and lower premenstrual syndrome levels. Single-blinded randomised controlled trial was used in the investigation, which involved 97 college students. Women over the age of 20 who were premenopausal made up the participants. The World Health Organization quality of life questionnaire (WHOQOL-BREF) and the Premenstrual Syndrome Scale (PMSS) were assessed using the music therapy and control groups. The women in the experimental groups had considerably lower premenstrual syndrome levels and better quality of life right after the intervention.

Auriculotherapy in the Cares to the Premenstrual Syndrome

The purpose of this study was to investigate at how auriculotherapy affected the severity of both physical and emotional PMS symptoms. Ninety-one women were randomly assigned to Auriculotherapy (AG), Placebo (PG), and Control (CG) groups. microneedles were placed in seven points related to PMS symptoms (Anxiety; Endocrine; Muscle relaxation; Analgesia; Kidney; Shen Men; and Sympathetic). AG and PG showed significantly lower scores of PMM symptoms, musculoskeletal pain, and quality of life and follow-up analysis, the significance was observed only in PG. To reduce the physical and emotional symptoms of PMS, auriculotherapy can be performed as an adjunctive therapy.
